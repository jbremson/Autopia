Game Guide

Objective

Autopia simulates a long term alternative fuel/vehicle transition. Players take the roles of consumers and vehicle producers. Each must evolve their behaviors as new regulatory and economic factors play out.

General FAQ

How do I sort tables on multiple columns?

To sort tables on multiple selected columns hold down the -shift- key while clicking on the table headings.

Consumer

The consumer player represents a market segment of drivers. Consumer segments have varying economic attributes and vehicle preferences. The first goal of the consumer is to make sure he has met his quota of vehicles. The second goal is to meet the preference requirements of the segment. These preferences are style, performance, mpg, and balance (i.e. bank balance).

The winning consumer player will make sure he meets his vehicle quota with the most attractive vehicles possible. The consumer negotiates with vehicle producers on prices and in order to improve the attractiveness of the vehicle selection.

Consumer FAQ

Q: How do I buy vehicles?

A:Vehicles are purchased using the "Buy Vehicles" screen. It is on the menu under "Global Screens." The funds for vehicle purchase come from an allowance given at the beggining of the turn.

Q: Can I negotiate vehicle prices with the vehicle producers?

A: Yes. Vehicle producers can change their prices.

Q: How do I know which vehicles are the most attractive?

A: The "score" column on the "Buy Vehicles" screen provides a measure of the relative attractiveness of a vehicle in terms of the existing models for sale. These scores can and will change during the turn based on various factors. For example, if the consumer overspends on his initial purchase (i.e. buys a vehicle outside of his typical price range) the score variable will increase for lower priced vehicles because meeting the consumer's quota maximizes his score.

Q: How do I buy fuel?

A: Fuel is purchased automatically at the beginning of the turn. The cost of the fuel is deducted from the vehicle purchasing allowance.

Q: Where can I see my current fleet?

A: See Private Screens -> My Fleet.

Q: Why do I lose vehicles from my fleet each turn?

A: This represents normal fleet attrition.

Q: How do I win?

A: The consumer who scores the most points is the winner of the consumer section of the game.

Q: Who is the v_init vehicle producer seen on the 'My Fleet' page ?

A: The v_init producer is used to set up the game scenario. It represents the consumer's existing fleet as the game begins.

Vehicle Producer

The vehicle producer represents a car company. His goal is to maximize his margin, market share and overall revenue by building and selling attractive vehicles, while meeting fuel economy regulations. The vehicle producer meets with consumers to negotiate vehicle sales and to find out what they want in the next generation of vehicles.

The vehicle producer must 1) create vehicles (using the Private Screen -> Create Vehicles link or by copying an existing vehicle on his Home screen) and 2) allocate R and D resources to shape his future vehicle attributes.

Vehicle Producer FAQ

Q: How do I know what the consumers want?

A: You must talk to the consumers. They know which vehicles are most attractive to them. Also, see the Consumer Info screen (at Global Screens -> Consumer Info) to learn about the consumer market segments.

Hint: The average price points are important information for designing attractive vehicles.

Q: How do I build vehicles?

A: You build vehicles using the Private Screen -> Build Vehicles link.

Q: What does it mean to copy a vehicle? How do I do it?

A: Copying a vehicle allows you to take an existing vehicle from the current turn and use it as a base for a vehicle produced on the next turn. It is a a convenience. Copy is a button on shown on the existing vehicles table in the Home screen.

Q: How do I change the price of a vehicle?

A: On the Home screen, click the link value for the vehicle price. The price change will take place immediately.

Q: How do I allocate R&D resources?

A: Use the Private Screens -> R&D Investment

Q: What happens if I don't sell all of the vehicles I build.

A: Remaining vehicles are sold off at 75% of your cost. Thus it is important to guage your market demand for a vehicle and make sure it gets sold, even if it requires a substantial discount.
